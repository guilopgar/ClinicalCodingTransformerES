A 47-year-old woman with morbid obesity, smoker.
Multiple hospital admissions, the last three months before death, due to respiratory superinfection.
A history of rheumatic fever in childhood.
She was diagnosed with long-standing arterial hypertension, cardiomyopathy of possible alcoholic origin, moderate aortic insufficiency, congestive heart failure, COPD and depressive syndrome.
Multiple hospitalizations related to obesity syndrome and associated hypoventilation, the last three months before death due to congestive heart failure and global respiratory failure exacerbated by over-infection.
In the first hours of the morning of her death, the family reported having respiratory distress and subsequently loss of consciousness.
After the emergency services have been informed and after applying advanced CPR maneuvers, they believe their death.
