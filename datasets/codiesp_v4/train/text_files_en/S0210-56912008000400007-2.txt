The patient was a 31-year-old male who suffered a motorcycle accident.
The chest X-ray showed mediastinal widening.
On admission, HTWCT showed a thoracic aorta pseudoaneurysm distal to the left subclavian artery exit with a significant periaortic and mediastinal hematoma associated with bilateral hemothorax.

She was admitted to the ICU where strict blood pressure control was established.
Eight hours after the trauma, an endoprosthesis was placed in the descending thoracic aorta via the left iliac artery.
The left subclavian artery was partially occluded without clinical repercussions.
After 24 hours, the patient was discharged to the hospital and one week later, he was discharged, as he was asymptomatic.
