3 years old patient (94 cm, 10 kg), girl, aphagous, with signs of malnutrition, who had second degree burns in anterior region and posterior right thigh, right flank and secondary mesentery (25% of surface).
Topical treatment was applied which was ineffective.
Due to the torpid evolution of the lesion, it was decided to perform a local mechanical debridement and application of argenic diazine dressing and TulgrasumÂ® scarring antibiotic impregnated bimixin sulfate (bacitracin).
Midazolam (1 mg i.m.) was administered prior to the procedure and 5 minutes later (50 mg i.m.).
Analgesia was achieved (grade 0 according to the verbal pain scale) and sedation was given at 10 minutes (score 4 on the Rami scale). Cure was achieved without incidents for 22 minutes.
The patient became sensitive and watched 120 minutes after the treatment.
The degree of amnesia of the surgical procedure assessed by the Korttila and Linnolia scale was total.
